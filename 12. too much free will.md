# too much free will

## can drive you insane

too much free will 

can drive you insane

book flights impulsively

to the sea you haven’t been in 13 years

dystopian pollution hides the buildings 

the national capital is jealous

wear imperfect blue stones

touch some grass

let the tattooed woman shoot you

change how you see yourself

the moon will make it better

meet some cats

eat fish and squid for dinner

you’re a human cat

get lost at the docks

tan beyond deep autumn 

be the unreliable narrator

sleep deprived and caffeinated

he’s the older brother you never had

she’s the beautiful woman

that didn’t figure out he likes her


golden sushi for prosperity

gift her a postcard of Mother 

and the waterfall we went to

the parents will fight all the time

until one of them is in the hospital

fake London for shopping alone

buy yourself silver for the dark winter 
meet you at the lake

you’re tall, brown, and blue

shut the box of strawberries with a hair clip

load up the film while the cat distracts

blow dandelions to the sun

make a lemonade

find your ripped up posters

wash out the highlights

listen to unreleased music

shoot you on a log

sing the lyrics to your song

a brutalist or a naturalist

should read the constitution

blind yourself with the flash

get her a jacket she will need

make it silent and dark

eat dinner with the spoon held wrong

shoot the shots and close the door

take a sip from the fountain one day


tourist on a Monday

drive up the hills 

the sea glitters from the mangroves

take off when you were supposed to land

the biome will change in an hour

watch the sunset twice from the same flight

it always rains when you go home

reunion at the conveyor belt

the yearning should fade

muses were never meant to stay
